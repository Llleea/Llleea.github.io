A comparison study: different modalities of movement in a precise VR environment

Group project from course Multimodal Interactions and Interfaces

Natural walk

CyberShoes

Controller

With 3 other students, we made up and conducted experiments on 8 volunteers to determine which of the modalities of movement between natural walk, the use of a controller and the use of CyberShoes is the more convenient for a VR simulation in a room-scale virtual environment, such as a virtual meeting room.
We set up a virtual environment in Unity where the participants had to achieve specific tasks. After the user tests, we compared the collected data to determine the strengths and weaknesses of the 3 modalities of locomotion.

  • Skills: Unity, C#, VR, user testing

Design of a community app for residents

Group project from course Media Technology and Interaction Design

Here is a a short video presentation of the app.

Homekey is an app allowing the residents of community housing such as student housing to get in touch with their neighbours and manage access to their building. A resident can give a temporary access to his building via the app.
In a group of 5 students, we went through the design process and build a prototype of the app.
